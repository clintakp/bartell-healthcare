DR. MICHAEL J. BARTELL

Clinic Director

DR. MICHAEL J. BARTELL, D.C. studied chiropractic at the New York Chiropractic College of the State University of New York. For the past 42 years of practice, Dr. Bartell has focused on clinical applications of the most current approaches to natural healthcare.

Dr. Bartell has acquired post-graduate expertise in applications of decompression therapy for lumbar spine pathologies, cold laser treatment of soft tissue and contiguous structural affections of the spine and joint structures, and clinical application of biomechanics and structural balance via custom-made, functional orthotics.

“Most satisfying to me are the capabilities available in the practice of chiropractic to integrate various natural approaches to synergistically achieve corrections in the body in a systemic sense.”

“My proudest accomplishment is the development of a clinically credible approach to cure adolescent idiopathic scoliosis that is non-restrictive and zero-bracing. My yet-to-be published findings regarding scoliosis detail my synergistic approach at treatment of this biomechanical, structural and biochemical affectation of over 1.5 million young people.”

“I welcome everyone to our clinic, understanding that we all deserve to live happy and healthy. I will do all I can to help us all to achieve this goal.”

DR SHERRIE MYERS BARTELL

Chief of Administration, policy Analysis, & Research

DR. SHERRIE MYERS BARTELL earned her Ph.D. in Public Administration from The Pennsylvania State University with emphases in learning organizations and public management. In addition, she holds an M.P.A. from The Pennsylvania State University; a B.A. degree in Mathematics from Long Island University; and professional certification in Training and Development from Penn State School of Behavioral Sciences and Education.

Dr. Sherrie joined the Bartell Healthcare Center in 2003 and serves as the principal liaison for the clinic, interfacing with insurance companies, patients, Managed Care Organizations, the Pennsylvania Department of Insurance, and many other external operatives relative to the practice of chiropractic in Pennsylvania. Much of her time is devoted to patient advocacy and staying abreast of developments in healthcare policy, both nationally and within Pennsylvania.

In 2007, Sherrie gave invited testimony to the Pennsylvania House Insurance Committee regarding two prospective bills. Sherrie’s publications include Principles & Practices of Public Administration (Marcel Dekker); Public Budgeting Laboratory Interactive Workbook (Carl Vinson Institute of Government); and Public Budgeting Laboratory Interactive Workbook User's Guide (Carl Vinson Institute of Government). In addition, she has had articles published in several peer-reviewed journals, including Innovations in Education and Training International, Public Administration Quarterly, International Journal of Organization Theory and Behavior, and Public Administration & Management.

When Dr. Sherrie is not working in the office, her hobbies include travel, knitting, and training her dogs, Zurie and Mishkah for Freestyle dance and other performance sports.
